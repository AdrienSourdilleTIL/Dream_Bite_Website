Consumed

A woman who can’t get enough.
Of gummies
Of wet dreams
Of Herself.

Three women, three writers, three insatiable appetites.

CONSUMED is a titillating queer drama that creeps into the unsettling corners of the female experience where the boundaries between body and mind start to blur. In the fevers of addiction, lucid dreams and self-cannibalism, these women are consumed by strange sounds, visions and desires. Time, space and identity warp around them. Are these intrusive fever dreams bringing them closer to their true selves ...or are they actually in danger of losing themselves forever?
Trailer


Made of three short plays, it has a through line of a woman descending further and further away from the real, and getting closer to her own body as a result. It originated by the three members of the collective writing a short piece in response to the prompt ‘altered states of mind’ that we brought together into a show as CONSUMED.

  • Gummy Gummy by Clare Stenning deals with drug addiction and the shame and thrill of substance abuse by creating on stage the protagonist’s inner world while she’s high.
  • Lucid by Katrina Bennett is a comedy exploring the disturbing fluidity of the relationship between friends, flatmate and lovers.
  • You Are What You Eat by Lydia Sabatini is a surreal body horror monologue about re-discovering the self through self-cannibalism (but funny).


CONSUMED is our first show, which had a sold-out week-long work-in-progress run at Camden People’s Theatre in October 2024. The run was ambitious, and praised in reviews for ‘unashamedly explor[ing] queerness and femininity in their messy, uncontained states, a kind of horror that derives its strength from just how real it could possibly be’ (there ought to be clowns).

We are excited about this show because it speaks to the very real uncertainties in which queer female bodies exist today, and the ways in which objectification, sexuality and financial instability can impact mental health and relationships. But rather than exploring these issues in a conventional, kitchen-sinky way we want to reach out into the audience’s guts in a visceral way, a show that sits in the body, as well as providing a great time for the audience.
